How I Think

Product principles shaping my work

The throughline across my projects is simple: when systems fail, the problem is often not capability, but how people interpret, trust, and use the system around them.

AI products fail when they ignore actual human behavior.
Good systems reduce friction without demanding attention.
Trust in AI is a UX problem before it is a model problem.
Robot products need social interaction design, not only hardware logic.
Next build direction

Next, I want a project that proves I can design human-robot trust and interaction flows.

The clearest gap in the current portfolio is a concrete robot or embodied-agent case. That should be the next addition: a project showing how people interpret timing, trust, interruption, and social cues from a machine in shared space.
